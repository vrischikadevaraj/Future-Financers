Budgeting Basics


Budgeting helps not only students, but many adults as well stay on track with their financial goals and teaches them how to be intentional with their money. Tracking income, expenses, and planning ahead can help avoid difficult situations and reduce financial stress. Building strong budgeting habits creates a good foundation and helps build good habits that will carry on throughout your life.


3 Steps to Budgeting


Income Tracking

Track all the money you get, such as allowance, part-time job money, birthday money, etc...
Visually seeing how much money you earn from different sources every month can help you when deciding what money you can realistically save and spend.

Expense Listing

Write out where you're spending your money and categorize them into fixed expenses, and variable expenses.
This can help you when deciding where you can cut back on spending and to help identify spending habits and patterns.

Planning

Create a spending plan that has an even mixture of needs, wants and savings.
Keep your tracker updated regularly and adjust as your income or spendings changes.
